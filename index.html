Hippocrates' Four Humors

About 2500 years ago, Hippocrates, called the "father of modern medicine" thought that a person's temperament came from the balance or excess of different bodily fluids.

  • Choleric
  • Melancholic
  • Sanguine
  • Phlegmatic

DISC Personality Types

In 1928, William Moulton Marston published Emotions of Normal People which became the four DISC personality types. This model has been widely marketed toward businesses as an aid in team-building and even as part of the application process for hiring.

  • Dominant
  • Inspiring
  • Supportive
  • Cautious

Myers-Briggs Type-Indicator

Also known as the Jung Personalities, the Myers-Briggs Type Inidicator was published in 1944. It outlined sets of traits personality traits, which combined to make 16 personalities. The traits were:

  • Extroversion/Introversion
  • Sensory/iNtuitive
  • Thinking/Feeling
  • Judging/Perceiving

Keirsey's Temperament-Sorter

Keirsey grouped the Myers-Briggs personality types into four temperaments based on common traits. In 1978, he published these temperaments in Please Understand Me, using the names of people's roles in society based on Plato's Republic.

  • Artisans
  • Guardians
  • Idealists
  • Rationals

Northwestern U's Personalities

In 2018, a group of researchers from Northwestern University did a global study using the NEO Personality Inventory. This includes five traits which have been widely recognized by modern psychologists including Jordan Peterson:

  • Neuroticism
  • Extraversion
  • Openness to new things
  • Agreeableness
  • Conscientiousness

The data they received based on their questionnaire showed that people fell into four basic clusters:

  • Average
  • Self-Centered
  • Role-Model
  • Reserved
